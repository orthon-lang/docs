<objective>
Add an LLM-native language research step to Phase 1 (Foundations, Process & Vision) in
`.planning/ROADMAP.md`, as a new success criterion whose deliverable is a written shortlist
of concrete concepts/ideas for strengthening Orthon's LLM-native design. Register this as
new requirement VISION-03 in `.planning/REQUIREMENTS.md`, and cross-reference the fact that
this shortlist feeds Phase 3's concept design and prioritization work.

currently only reflected in exploratory notes (`docs/notes/llm-generability-gate.md`,
`docs/notes/language-llm-comparison.md`) and the LLM Generability Gate (EDR-011). Nothing in
the roadmap makes a concrete research step for "what makes a language LLM-native" that
directly informs which candidate concepts/patterns get prioritized when the 13 outstanding
concepts are designed in Phase 3. This quick task closes that gap at the roadmap-planning
level only — it does not perform the research itself (that happens during Phase 1 execution).

Output: `.planning/ROADMAP.md` Phase 1 gains a 6th success criterion (LLM-native research →
concept shortlist, cross-referencing Phase 3) and VISION-03 in its Requirements line; Phase
3's Goal gains a short acknowledgment that it consumes that shortlist.
`.planning/REQUIREMENTS.md` gains the VISION-03 requirement definition, traceability row,
and updated coverage/summary counts (56 → 57 v1 requirements).
</objective>
